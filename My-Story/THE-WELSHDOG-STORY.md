# 🐾 The WelshDog Story — How a Neurodivergent Kid From Wales Built His Dream

*Written June 2026 — Raw, real, unfiltered*

---

## 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁷󠁬󠁳󠁿 Chapter 1 — Before The Code

Before any of this existed, I was just a Welsh lad with a brain that never quite worked the way the world expected it to.

Dyslexia. ADHD. The kind of brain that teachers didn't really know what to do with. The kind that hyper-fixates at 2am on something nobody else cares about, then can't remember where it put its keys.

I spent 9 years working in pubs and hospitality. Good crack, genuinely. You learn people. You learn how to read a room, how to hustle, how to keep going when you're running on empty. But deep down I always knew I was supposed to be building something. I just didn't know what yet.

I wasn't supposed to be a developer. No CS degree. No bootcamp. No mentor handing me a roadmap. Just me, a laptop, YouTube, and a brain that refused to stop until it figured things out.

That's the neurodivergent superpower nobody talks about — when you finally find *the thing*, nothing in the world can stop you.

I found my thing.

---

## 🤖 Chapter 2 — The BROski Clan Is Born (2021)

January 2021. I'm building a Discord server. Not because someone told me to. Not because there was a business plan. Just because I wanted a place where people like me could hang out, learn stuff, and feel like they belonged.

I called it **The BROski Clan**.

From day one I was in the tool-room at 17:07 on a Tuesday typing `!help` into a bot, figuring out every command, setting up auto-moderation, configuring prefixes. I changed the bot prefix to `bs` — BROski shorthand — because of course I did.

The server had channels for everything:
- Web3 and airdrops
- Crypto education
- Gaming
- Anti-scam protection
- Bot rooms
- Rain rooms

I was building community infrastructure before I even knew that's what it was called.

Then in September 2021 something properly wild happened. I minted **The BROski Token** on MintMe. NFT ID 23, Symbol MPN. Transferred it live. Logged the whole thing in Discord in real time. Gas: 0.000071546 Gwei.

A Welsh lad from Llanelli. No formal training. Minting tokens and piping on-chain data into his own Discord server.

The dream was already alive. I just hadn't named it yet.

## 💙 Chapter 3 — WelshDog House & The Dream Takes Shape (2024)

By 2024 things were getting clearer.

I built **WelshDog House** — a personal Discord server, my own space. The important information channel had sections for:

- AI Show And Tell
- Dyslexia and ADHD support
- Game Zone
- WelshDog Zone

The sign-off read: *"Get ready to wag your digital tails, explore, and make this home yours. Welcome to WelshDog Home — where every bark matters."*

And then on **7 February 2024**, I wrote this in the server:

> *"Hyperfocus Studio, our dream is simple: a space where neurodivergent creativity knows no bounds."*

That was it. That was the whole mission in one sentence. Written in my own Discord, to nobody in particular, just because it was true.

I wasn't pitching to investors. I wasn't writing a grant application. I was just being honest about what I wanted to build.

A safe space. A powerful space. A space that works the way neurodivergent brains actually work — in bursts, in obsessions, in hyperfocus sprints at weird hours with snacks and lo-fi beats.

## ⚡ Chapter 4 — Building in Hyperfocus (2025–2026)

This is where it got serious.

The AI stack came together fast once I found my rhythm. We're talking:

- **HyperCode-V2.4** — the core backend with agents, services, Redis, PostgreSQL, Ollama running local models
- **BROski-Obsidian-Brain** — knowledge management, Graph Brain, AI orchestration baked into my own vault
- **HyperFocus Zone Portal** — the main web interface for the whole ecosystem
- **Hyper-Vibe-Coding-Course** — a full course platform with Stripe integration
- **HYPER-SKILLS-Registry** — skill catalogue powering agent routing
- **MCP Gateway** — Model Context Protocol bridge tying all the agents together

The BROski Bot went from a UnbelievaBoat prefix config in 2021 to a full AI agent with:
- Graph-aware RAG morning briefings hitting Discord at 7am
- Brain Citations pulling from real vault notes
- Skill routing via `/route` — agents picking the best tools automatically
- The Brain Constellation at 192 nodes — skills, code, notes all interconnected

And the crew orchestrator? Every `/execute` call now checks the Brain, matches HYPER-SILLs, injects them into the agent prompt. That's not a bot anymore. That's a nervous system.

All of it self-taught. All of it hyperfocus-built. Most of it done late at night in Llanelli, Wales, with Docker logs scrolling in one terminal and Claude in another.

## 🌍 Chapter 5 — Why It All Exists

Here's the real talk.

I'm building HyperFocus Zone because I was the kid who slipped through the cracks. The one the system didn't have a box for. Dyslexic. ADHD. Too much energy in the wrong direction, not enough support pointed at the right ones.

And I know I'm not alone. There are thousands — millions — of people with brains like mine who are sitting on something incredible but can't get traction because the tools, the systems, the education, the workplaces — none of it was built for them.

So I'm building it myself.

HyperFocus Zone is open-source because talent doesn't care where you grew up or what diagnosis you got in school. It should be free to access. It should feel like it was built by someone who *gets it* — because it was.

Every feature. Every agent. Every Discord notification. Every Brain Citation in a morning briefing. It's all pointing at the same thing:

**What if the tools actually worked the way your brain does?**
